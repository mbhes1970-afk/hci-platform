Target Market Definition

Define exactly where you will compete. Geography, verticals, company size, and regulatory drivers. No broad strokes — surgical precision.

Market Canvas
Primary Geography
Which EU countries first? E.g. DACH, Benelux, Nordics. Consider language, regulation maturity, existing contacts.
Target Verticals
Max 3 verticals. E.g. Healthcare, Financial Services, Critical Infrastructure. Based on product fit + regulatory urgency.
Company Size & Profile
Enterprise (1000+ FTE), Mid-market (250-1000), or SMB? Revenue range, IT maturity level, decision structure.
Regulatory Drivers
Which regulations create urgency? CRA, NIS2, DORA, AI Act. Map regulation → vertical → timeline.
Market Size Estimate
TAM/SAM/SOM for your defined segment. How many target accounts exist in this intersection?
ICP Scoring Criteria
  • Firmographic Match (40%) — Right geography, vertical, size, and IT maturity. Score A/B/C based on exact overlap with your ideal profile.
  • Regulatory Pressure (25%) — Active compliance deadline within 12 months. Board-level awareness. Budget allocated or allocatable.
  • Accessible Decision Maker (20%) — Can you reach the CISO, CTO, or compliance officer? Existing network, LinkedIn connection, event overlap?
  • Technology Fit (15%) — Compatible tech stack, no incumbent lock-in, integration complexity manageable within Sprint A timeframe.
💡
HCI Tip: Start with 50 accounts maximum in your ICP-A tier. Depth beats breadth. You need 3-5 real conversations, not 500 cold emails. The HCI database covers 6,339 contacts across 12 EU industries.

Core Problem Identification

What pain does your target market feel today? Not your feature list — their sleepless nights. Quantified, urgent, and recognizable in their language.

Problem Statement Canvas
Primary Pain Point
The #1 problem in their words. Not your solution description — their frustration. E.g. "We have no visibility into our NIS2 compliance gaps."
Business Impact
Quantify the cost of inaction. Hours lost, fines risked, revenue at stake, competitive disadvantage. Use their KPIs.
Current Alternative
What do they do today? Excel sheets, manual audits, consulting firms, ignore it? Why is that inadequate?
Urgency Trigger
What forces action NOW? Regulatory deadline, board pressure, incident, competitor move, audit finding?
Problem Validation Checklist
  • Real conversations (min 5) — Have you spoken to actual prospects? Not assumed pain — validated pain from discovery calls or assessment responses.
  • Quantified impact — Can you put a € number on the problem? Cost of breach, hours per week, FTE equivalent, regulatory fine potential.
  • Budget exists or is creatable — Is there a line item, or can you help them build the business case? Compliance budgets are often easier to unlock.
  • Decision maker cares — Does the C-level recognize this problem? Board reports, strategic priorities, public statements that confirm relevance.
  • Timing is right — Is the regulatory deadline approaching? Has there been a recent incident in their sector? Market window validation.

Value Proposition Formulation

Your PMC value formula: who you help, what problem you solve, how you're different, and why they should act now. One sentence that opens doors.

Value Proposition Formula
For [Target Customer]
Specific role + organization type. E.g. "For CISOs at mid-market healthcare organizations in DACH"
Who [Have This Problem]
The validated pain from Step 2. In their language, not yours.
Our [Solution] Provides
What you deliver — outcome-focused, not feature-focused. "Continuous NIS2 compliance monitoring" not "dashboard with 47 features"
Unlike [Alternative]
What they use today (from Step 2). Why your approach is fundamentally better, not incrementally better.
We [Unique Differentiator]
Your unfair advantage. EU-sovereign, AI-powered, 10x faster, proven in sector, specific integration. One thing they can't get elsewhere.
Elevator Pitch Variants
10-Second Version (Networking)
One sentence. "We help [who] solve [problem] through [approach]." No jargon.
30-Second Version (Cold Call)
Problem → solution → proof → CTA. "Organizations like [reference] faced [problem]. We helped them [result]. Could we explore if this applies to you?"
2-Minute Version (Meeting Opener)
Context → problem → approach → differentiator → ask. Structured but conversational. End with a question, not a pitch.
💡
HCI Tip: Test your value proposition with the "So What?" test. After every statement, ask "So what?" from the prospect's perspective. If you can't answer in business impact terms, rewrite it.

Offer Packaging

Package your solution into Sprint A-B-C tiers. Low barrier entry, clear deliverables, predictable investment. Make it easy to say yes.

Sprint A-B-C Model
Sprint A — Quick Win (4-6 weeks)
Low investment entry point. Assessment, proof-of-concept, or pilot. What can you deliver in 30 days that proves value? €5K-15K range.
Sprint B — Implementation (3-6 months)
Full deployment based on Sprint A findings. Measurable outcomes, defined scope, clear milestones. €25K-75K range.
Sprint C — Scale & Optimize (ongoing)
Expansion, optimization, managed service. Recurring revenue model. Annual contract with quarterly reviews. €50K-150K/year range.
Offer Design Principles
  • Sprint A must stand alone — Deliver real value even if they never buy Sprint B. An assessment report, compliance gap analysis, or architecture review has standalone worth.
  • Clear deliverables per sprint — No ambiguity. List exactly what they receive: reports, dashboards, configurations, training sessions, documentation.
  • Risk reversal — Money-back on Sprint A if no value delivered? Shared risk model? The lower the barrier, the faster the decision.
  • Pricing anchored to impact — Price against the cost of the problem, not against your costs. A €10K assessment that prevents a €500K fine is cheap.
  • Upsell path is natural — Sprint A naturally reveals the need for Sprint B. Design it that way. The assessment uncovers gaps → implementation fills them.

Partnership Alignment

Align your go-to-market with local partners, channel strategy, and HCI's network. You don't enter Europe alone — you enter with leverage.

Partnership Canvas
Local Channel Partners
Which resellers, integrators, or consultancies can you partner with? Per geography and vertical. Existing relationships?
Technology Partners
Microsoft, AWS, or other platform partnerships? Marketplace listings, co-sell programs, technology alliances?
HCI Network Activation
Which HCI contacts and relationships are relevant? C-level introductions, industry events, advisory board connections?
Go-to-Market Model
Direct sales, partner-led, or hybrid? Commission structure, deal registration, territory assignments?
Revenue Share & Terms
How do you split revenue with partners? Referral fee, reseller margin, co-sell commission? Contractual framework.
PMC Readiness Checklist
  • Target market defined — Geography, verticals, company profile, and ICP scoring criteria documented. Top-50 accounts identified.
  • Core problem validated — Quantified pain point confirmed through real conversations or benchmark data. Business impact in € terms.
  • Value proposition sharp — One-sentence formula tested. Elevator pitch variants ready for networking, cold call, and meeting contexts.
  • Offer packaged in sprints — Sprint A/B/C defined with clear deliverables, timelines, and pricing. Sprint A has standalone value.
  • Partnerships mapped — Local channel, technology partners, and HCI network activation plan ready. Revenue model agreed.
  • PMC document created — All five building blocks combined into a single reference document for the GTM phase.
  • Ready for GTM Preparation (Phase 2) — All inputs available for pitch deck, CRM setup, and campaign creation.
