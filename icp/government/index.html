Procurement Reality

How public sector actually buys technology

Knowing the market size is irrelevant if you don't understand how decisions are made. Public procurement is a process — not a conversation.

Phase 1 · Awareness
The accountability question comes first
Municipal councils increasingly ask their boards: how digitally mature are we? Are we NIS2 compliant? What is our AI governance? The question precedes the budget. Technology that answers the accountability question gets on the agenda.
"We position your product as the answer to the question that's already being asked."
Phase 2 · Orientation
Independent validation is essential
Public organisations are deeply sceptical of vendor-led assessments. They trust independent analysis. An introduction via a respected independent source carries 10x the weight of cold outreach from a vendor.
"HCI's 30 years of government relationships is the introduction that gets you into the room."
Phase 3 · Selection
Aanbestedingsplicht changes everything
Above certain thresholds, Dutch municipalities must publicly tender. This is not a barrier — it is a calendar. Know when the tender will open, position yourself before it does, and be the known quantity when evaluation begins.
"We track tender cycles and pre-position your product before procurement teams write the spec."
Phase 4 · Reference
One G4 reference opens the G40
Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Den Haag, Utrecht. One G4 municipality as reference customer dramatically accelerates adoption in the G40 and beyond. The reference strategy must be designed from day one — not discovered after month 18.
"Your first public sector customer is your most important sales asset. We select it deliberately."

Regulatory Drivers

Three laws creating immediate demand

These are not future requirements. They are active obligations creating budget and urgency in every public organisation in the Netherlands right now.

BIO · Baseline Informatiebeveiliging Overheid
Mandatory information security for all government
Every Dutch public organisation must demonstrate BIO compliance. Not optional. Not voluntary. Products that help municipalities measure, demonstrate and improve BIO compliance have a built-in mandatory market driver.
NIS2 · In force 2025
Active accountability for digital resilience
Municipalities must demonstrate active digital security controls and report incidents. Councils are now asking their boards to explain their NIS2 status. Technology that provides the answer — or the evidence — is in demand.
EU AI Act · Governance requirement
AI governance before AI deployment
Municipalities are deploying AI tools for citizen services, permit handling and fraud detection. The EU AI Act requires governance frameworks before deployment. Digital maturity assessment is the logical starting point — and HCI's entry.
