01 — Why 90% fail

Five mistakes.
Same outcome: burned budget, no customers.

Every failed EU market entry we've seen makes at least three of these. They're predictable — and preventable.

MISTAKE #0 — THE ROOT CAUSE

Skipping validation and going straight to sales

"We have a great product, we just need someone to sell it in Europe." This is the number one killer. Without validated proposition-market fit for EU buyers, everything that follows is wasted budget. European procurement is different. Decision cycles are longer. Compliance is table stakes.

MISTAKE #1

No EU legal entity

European enterprise buyers cannot procure from a non-EU entity without significant legal risk. GDPR requires an EU representative. No entity = invisible to procurement.

MISTAKE #2

Compliance as afterthought

GDPR, NIS2, CRA — these aren't obstacles. They're buying criteria. EU buyers check compliance before they check features. If you're not compliant, you're not in the conversation.

MISTAKE #3

Hiring before validating

Hiring a country manager before validating your proposition is like hiring a pilot before building the plane. You need product-market combination first, then hire for execution.

MISTAKE #4

No local network

C-level trust in Europe is earned through relationships, not cold outreach. Without a warm network in Benelux or DACH, your sales cycle doubles — or never starts.
