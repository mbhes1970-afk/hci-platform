Six Dimensions

What we're actually measuring

Each dimension has three questions. Each question has four answer options weighted 0-25. Together they give a composite score that reflects real EU market readiness — not surface-level optimism.

01
ICP Clarity
Can you describe your ideal European customer in one sentence — sector, company size, job title, specific pain? EU enterprise buyers require precision. A vague ICP means no introductions.
Questions: Target definition · EU buyer profile · Decision-maker mapping
02
Regulatory Compliance
Which EU regulations apply to your product, and what is your current compliance status? GDPR, NIS2, AI Act, CRA, DORA, NEN 7510 — each has commercial implications before the first meeting.
Questions: Applicable regulations · Current status · Roadmap
03
Product-Market Fit EU
Does your product solve a problem that EU buyers recognise — in language they use? US metrics like HCAHPS or FTE savings may need reframing around compliance, sovereignty or workforce efficiency.
Questions: Problem recognition · EU value drivers · Local references
04
GTM Model
Direct, indirect or hybrid? Your channel model depends on product type, price point, sector and market maturity. The wrong model doesn't just slow you down — it burns introductions.
Questions: Channel strategy · Partner model · Sales cycle design
05
Team Readiness
Who manages the EU relationship on your side? EU buyers expect continuity, local language capability and someone with decision-making authority accessible in their timezone.
Questions: EU point of contact · Language · Decision authority
06
Financial Runway
EU enterprise sales cycles run 6–18 months. Each programme runs per quarter — no long-term commitment required upfront. You evaluate results at the end of each quarter before renewing.
Questions: Runway duration · Budget allocated · Board commitment
